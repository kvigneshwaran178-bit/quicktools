Best Practices

Tips for Better QR Codes

Make your QR codes scannable, durable, and effective.

🎯

Keep Content Short

Shorter URLs and text create simpler QR patterns that are faster to scan — especially on low-res prints.

🔲

High Contrast Colors

Always use a dark foreground on a white background. Low contrast causes scan failures in bright light.

📐

Print at the Right Size

For print, use at least 2cm × 2cm. For billboards or banners, use the maximum 512px export size.

🛡️

Use High Error Correction

H-level error correction lets QR codes still scan even if up to 30% of the code is damaged or covered.

📱

Test Before Printing

Always scan your QR code on at least two different phones before printing hundreds of copies.

🔗

Use URL Shorteners

Long URLs create dense QR patterns. Shorten them with Bitly or similar before generating your code.
