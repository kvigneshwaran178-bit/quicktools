How It Works

BMI Formula Explained

Body Mass Index (BMI) is calculated by dividing your weight by the square of your height. It is a simple, widely-used screening index, first developed by Adolphe Quetelet in the 1830s.

Metric: BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²)
Imperial: BMI = 703 × Weight (lbs) ÷ Height² (inches²)

For example, a person who is 170 cm (1.70 m) tall and weighs 65 kg has a BMI of 65 ÷ (1.70 × 1.70) = 22.5 — which falls in the Normal category.

Worked Examples

BMI Calculation Examples

BMI = 58 ÷ (1.63)² = 58 ÷ 2.66

BMI = 21.8 — Normal Weight ✅

Fitness Guidance

Tips to Achieve a Healthy BMI

🥗

Eat Balanced Meals

Focus on whole grains, lean proteins, vegetables, and healthy fats. Limit ultra-processed foods and sugary drinks.

🏃

Stay Physically Active

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week. Even daily 30-minute walks make a big difference.

💧

Drink Enough Water

Adequate hydration boosts metabolism, reduces overeating, and supports healthy body function. Target 8 glasses a day.

😴

Prioritise Sleep

Poor sleep disrupts hunger hormones and increases cravings. Adults need 7–9 hours of quality sleep per night.

🧘

Manage Stress

Chronic stress raises cortisol, which can lead to weight gain. Meditation, yoga, and mindfulness help keep it in check.

🩺

Regular Health Check-ups

Monitor your vitals, blood work, and weight with a healthcare provider. Prevention is always better than cure.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple numerical index calculated from height and weight. It is widely used by healthcare professionals as a first-line screening tool to identify potential weight-related health risks such as heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and hypertension. While not a diagnostic tool, it provides a useful population-level indicator.
For most adults, a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered healthy. Below 18.5 indicates underweight status, 25–29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above falls into the obese category. For South Asian and Indian populations, health authorities recommend a lower healthy threshold of 18.5–22.9 due to higher metabolic risk at lower BMI values.
The BMI formula is the same for both men and women, but the interpretation can differ slightly. Women naturally have a higher body fat percentage than men at the same BMI. Some clinicians account for this, but the standard WHO categories apply to both sexes equally in general practice.
Yes. BMI does not distinguish between muscle and fat. A highly muscular athlete can have a high BMI while having very low body fat — which would be misclassified as overweight. For a more accurate assessment, consider body fat percentage measurements, waist-to-hip ratio, or DEXA scans alongside BMI.
Studies show South Asians, including Indians, experience increased metabolic health risks (like diabetes and cardiovascular disease) at lower BMI values compared to Caucasian populations. The Indian Council of Medical Research recommends a healthy BMI range of 18–23 for Indians, with overweight defined at 23–24.9 and obesity at 25 and above.
Standard BMI categories are designed for adults (20+). For children and adolescents (2–19 years), BMI is interpreted using age- and sex-specific percentiles, known as BMI-for-age. This calculator is designed for adult use only. Please consult a paediatrician for children's weight assessment.
