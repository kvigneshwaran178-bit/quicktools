What is a SIP Investment?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly — typically monthly — into a mutual fund scheme. Rather than investing a lump sum, SIP lets you invest small amounts over time, making wealth creation accessible to everyone.

SIP leverages two powerful financial concepts: Rupee Cost Averaging (you buy more units when markets are low and fewer when high) and Power of Compounding (your returns generate their own returns over time). Over long periods, even a small monthly SIP can create substantial wealth.

⚡

Rupee Cost Averaging

By investing the same amount monthly, you automatically buy more units when prices fall and fewer when they rise — reducing your average cost per unit over time.

🔁

Power of Compounding

Your investment returns are reinvested to generate their own returns. The longer you invest, the more exponential the growth — time is your most valuable asset.

📐

Disciplined Investing

SIP instills financial discipline. An auto-debit mandate means you invest before you spend — the fundamental rule of wealth creation.

🛡️

Market Volatility Buffer

Unlike lump sum investments, SIP spreads your entry points across time, making you less vulnerable to the risk of investing at market peaks.

Formula

How is SIP Return Calculated?

Our SIP calculator uses the standard Future Value of Annuity formula used by financial institutions and mutual fund companies across India:

SIP Maturity Formula
M = P × {[(1 + r)ⁿ − 1] ÷ r} × (1 + r)
M = Maturity Amount (total corpus at the end) P = Monthly SIP investment amount (₹) r = Monthly rate of return = Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100 n = Total number of months = Years × 12

For SIP with Annual Step-Up

When you increase your SIP amount by a fixed percentage every year, each year's contribution is calculated separately using the base formula and summed. This better reflects real-world SIP behaviour where your income and investments grow over time.

Inflation-Adjusted Real Value

The real (inflation-adjusted) value of your maturity amount is: Real Value = Maturity Amount ÷ (1 + inflation rate)ⁿ where n is the number of years. This tells you what your money will actually be worth in today's purchasing power.

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)

CAGR represents the annualised growth of your investment as a single percentage: CAGR = ((Maturity / Total Invested)^(1/n) − 1) × 100. This helps compare SIP performance against other investment options like FD, gold, or real estate.

A SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) allows you to invest a fixed amount in a mutual fund at regular intervals — typically monthly. It helps build wealth over time through the power of compounding and rupee cost averaging, and is one of the most disciplined ways to invest in equity markets.
SIP maturity is calculated using the Future Value of Annuity formula: M = P × {[(1 + r)ⁿ – 1] / r} × (1 + r), where M = Maturity Amount, P = Monthly SIP amount, r = Monthly rate of return (Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n = Total months (Years × 12).

Historically, large-cap equity mutual funds in India have delivered 10–12% CAGR, mid-cap funds 12–15%, and small-cap funds 15–18% over long periods. Debt funds typically return 6–8%. For long-term equity SIP calculations, 10–12% is considered a realistic and conservative estimate. Avoid using overly optimistic rates for planning.
A SIP Step-Up (or top-up SIP) means increasing your monthly SIP amount by a fixed percentage every year — for example, 10% annually. As your income grows, increasing your SIP proportionally can dramatically boost your final corpus. A ₹5,000 SIP with a 10% annual step-up over 20 years at 12% returns can give nearly 2× the corpus of a flat ₹5,000 SIP.
Inflation erodes the purchasing power of your returns. For example, ₹1 crore after 20 years at 6% inflation is worth only about ₹31 lakh in today's money. Our calculator shows the real (inflation-adjusted) value of your maturity amount so you can understand your true wealth gain and plan accordingly for goals like retirement or education.
