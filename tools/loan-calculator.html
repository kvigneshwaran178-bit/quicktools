The Math

How EMI is Calculated

Our loan calculator uses the standard banking formula adopted by all Indian banks and NBFCs.

EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / [ (1+r)ⁿ – 1 ]
P
Principal loan amount — the amount you borrow from the bank
r
Monthly interest rate = Annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100
n
Number of months (loan tenure in months)
EMI
Equated Monthly Instalment — fixed amount paid each month

Smart Tips

How to Reduce Your Loan EMI

Practical tips to lower your monthly outgo and save on interest.

💳

Improve Your Credit Score

A CIBIL score above 750 helps you negotiate lower interest rates, directly reducing your EMI.

🏦

Compare Multiple Lenders

Even a 0.5% difference in rate on a ₹50L loan saves over ₹1.5L in total interest. Always compare.

📅

Choose the Right Tenure

Longer tenure = lower EMI but more total interest. Use this calculator to find your sweet spot.

💰

Make a Bigger Down Payment

Every extra rupee you put down reduces principal, which directly reduces both your EMI and total interest.

🔄

Part-Prepay When Possible

Making even one extra payment per year can cut your tenure significantly and save lakhs in interest.

🔁

Balance Transfer

If you have a high-rate loan, consider transferring it to a lender offering 2–3% lower rates.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Instalment. It is the fixed amount you pay to the bank or lender every month until the loan is fully repaid. Each EMI covers a portion of the principal and the accrued interest for that month. In the early months, a larger share goes toward interest; as time progresses, more goes toward principal.
Loan EMI is calculated using the standard formula: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n ÷ [(1+r)^n – 1], where P is the principal, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the loan tenure in months. Our calculator uses this exact formula to give you accurate results.
Personal loan interest rates in India typically range from 10% to 24% per annum depending on the lender, borrower's credit score, monthly income, and employer profile. Rates below 13% are generally considered competitive. PSU banks like SBI tend to offer lower rates (10–14%) compared to private banks or NBFCs (14–24%).
Yes! You have several options: (1) Make part-prepayments to reduce the outstanding principal, which either lowers the EMI or shortens the tenure. (2) Negotiate a lower rate with your bank if rates have dropped. (3) Do a balance transfer to a lender offering a lower rate. Most banks in India allow part-prepayment without penalty after a lock-in period.
Most banks in India use FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio). They typically allow total EMIs up to 40–50% of your net monthly income. So if your take-home is ₹60,000/month, you can service EMIs of up to ₹24,000–₹30,000. The loan amount you qualify for depends on this EMI capacity combined with the interest rate and chosen tenure.
Under a flat rate, interest is calculated on the original principal throughout the loan tenure, making it more expensive. Under reducing balance rate (used by most Indian banks today), interest is calculated only on the outstanding principal each month — so as you repay, you pay less interest over time. Our EMI calculator uses the reducing balance method, which is the standard for all Indian banks and NBFCs.
