📐 How Compression Works

Understanding PDF Compression

PDF compression reduces file size by removing redundant data, downsampling embedded images, compressing font streams, and stripping unnecessary metadata without altering your document's content or layout.

Compression Ratio = (Original Size − Compressed Size) ÷ Original Size × 100%

Space Saved = Original Size − Compressed Size

Example: 5 MB → 1.8 MB = 64% compression ratio

Our tool applies lossless compression on text/vector elements and configurable lossy compression on embedded images — giving you precise control over the quality vs. size trade-off.

💡 Compression Tips

Get the Best Results

🖼️

Image-Heavy PDFs

Use Medium or High compression with quality at 60–75% to dramatically reduce photo-heavy documents.

📝

Text-Only Documents

Even without images, lossless compression removes metadata bloat — use Low compression for best fidelity.

🖨️

Scanned PDFs

Scanned files are essentially images. Use High compression + 55–65% quality to shrink them significantly.

📧

Email Attachments

Most email servers limit attachments to 10–25 MB. Aim for <5 MB using High compression.

🌐

Website PDFs

PDFs served on websites should be under 2 MB for fast loading. Use High compression accordingly.

📱

Mobile Sharing

Keep under 5 MB for smooth sharing via WhatsApp, Telegram, or social media platforms.
