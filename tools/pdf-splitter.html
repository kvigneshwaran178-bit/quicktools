About PDF Splitting

A PDF (Portable Document Format) file is a container that can hold multiple pages, each as an independent unit. Splitting a PDF means extracting one or more pages from the container and saving them as a new, standalone PDF document.

This is useful when you need to share only specific pages of a report, extract an invoice from a batch file, or break a large scanned document into smaller, manageable parts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this PDF splitter free to use? ▼
Yes, our PDF Splitter is completely free with no hidden charges, no subscription, and no sign-up required. Just open the tool and start splitting.
Is my PDF file uploaded to any server? ▼
No. Your PDF is processed entirely inside your browser using PDF.js and the PDF-lib library. No file is ever uploaded to our servers. Your document stays 100% private on your device.
What is the maximum PDF file size supported? ▼
There is no hard file size limit imposed by our tool. The tool works with any PDF size your browser can handle. For very large files (over 200MB) performance depends on your device's available RAM and browser capabilities.
Can I extract specific pages from a PDF? ▼
Yes. You can enter custom page ranges like 1-3, 5, 7-9 to extract exactly the pages you need. You can also visually click thumbnails to select pages, or use the "Split Each Page" mode to separate every page into its own file.
What browsers support this PDF Splitter? ▼
Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari, and all modern browsers are supported. Mobile browsers on Android and iOS are also fully supported, making this a great mobile PDF splitter.
Can I split scanned PDFs? ▼
Yes. Scanned PDFs are just images embedded in a PDF container, so splitting works perfectly — the scanned images are preserved exactly as-is without any quality loss.
Will the split PDFs lose quality? ▼
No. Pages are extracted at full quality without any re-compression or rendering. Text, images, fonts, and all embedded content are preserved perfectly in the output files.
Can I split password-protected PDFs? ▼
Currently, this tool supports unlocked (non-password-protected) PDFs. If your PDF has a password, you will need to remove the password protection first before uploading.
