The Math

Discount Formulas Explained

Simple, accurate formulas used by our calculator for every mode.

Sale Price from % Off
Sale Price = MRP × (1 – D/100)
Where D is the discount percentage. Multiply by the complement of the discount rate.
Discount Amount
Savings = MRP – Sale Price
The actual rupee amount you save, simply the difference between original and final price.
Discount Percentage
D% = (Savings ÷ MRP) × 100
The proportion of the original price you're saving, expressed as a percentage.
Original Price (Reverse)
MRP = Sale Price ÷ (1 – D/100)
Work backwards from sale price and known discount to find the original MRP.
Double Discount
Final = MRP × (1–D1/100) × (1–D2/100)
Stacked discounts multiply, they don't add. Always less than the sum of both discounts.
Effective Single Discount
Deff = D1 + D2 – (D1×D2/100)
The true combined discount rate when two discounts are applied successively.

Smart Shopping

Tips to Get the Best Discounts

Make the most of every sale with these smart buying strategies.

🎯

Compare Across Platforms

A 40% discount on an inflated MRP may be worse than a 15% discount on a genuine price. Always check multiple sellers.

📅

Best Times to Buy

Festival sales (Diwali, Big Billion Day, Amazon Sale) offer 30–70% genuine discounts. Bookmark items before the sale to track real discounts.

💳

Stack Bank Offers

Combine site discounts with bank card offers for extra 5–10% off. This is a true stacked discount — our calculator handles this!

🔢

Understand Real Savings

25% off doesn't feel the same on a ₹100 item vs a ₹10,000 item. Always calculate the actual rupee savings before deciding.

⚠️

Watch for Inflated MRP

Some sellers inflate MRP to show bigger discount percentages. Check the item's price history using tools like Keepa or PriceHistory.app.

📦

Bulk Discount Value

For business purchases, use our double discount mode to calculate tiered pricing — e.g. 15% trade discount + 5% cash discount combined.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

To calculate discount percentage, use: Discount % = (Discount Amount ÷ Original Price) × 100. For example, if a ₹500 item is sold at ₹400, the discount is ₹100, and the discount percentage is (100 ÷ 500) × 100 = 20%. Use the "Amount Saved → %" mode in our calculator for this instantly.
Sale Price = Original Price × (1 – Discount% ÷ 100). For a 30% discount on a ₹1,000 item: Sale Price = 1000 × (1 – 30/100) = 1000 × 0.70 = ₹700. So you pay ₹700 and save ₹300. Use the "% Off → Sale Price" mode in our calculator.
Discount amount is the actual rupee value saved (e.g., ₹150 off), while discount percentage is the proportion saved relative to the original price (e.g., 15% off). Both matter: the rupee amount tells you the real financial saving, while the percentage allows easy comparison across products at different price points.
Original Price = Sale Price ÷ (1 – Discount% ÷ 100). So if a sale price is ₹700 and the discount was 30%, then Original Price = 700 ÷ (1 – 0.30) = 700 ÷ 0.70 = ₹1,000. Use the "Find Original Price" mode in our calculator to do this automatically.
For stacked discounts (e.g., 20% off then 10% off), the combined discount is NOT simply 30%. The formula is: Final Price = Original × (1 – D1/100) × (1 – D2/100). On ₹1,000 with 20% then 10% off: 1000 × 0.80 × 0.90 = ₹720, saving 28% total — not 30%. Use our "Double Discount" mode to calculate this precisely.
Discounts of 10–20% are common for regular sales in India. Festival or clearance sales typically offer 30–50%. Discounts above 60% are rare and may signal very old stock or an inflated original MRP. Always verify the sale price against other platforms before purchasing to confirm the discount is genuine.
